# T3 Brief: Chemical Composition in Planet Formation + PLATO Context

## Goal
Survey how chemical composition is tracked in planet formation models, the PLATO mission context, and how chemistry couples to dynamics. Write findings to `outputs/.drafts/planet-formation-simulations-state-research-T3.md`.

## Key Topics to Cover

1. **Chemical composition models in planet formation:**
   - Condensation sequences and equilibrium chemistry in protoplanetary disks
   - Ice lines (water, CO, CO2, etc.) and their role in setting planet composition
   - Volatile transport and vapor enrichment models
   - Key frameworks: chemcomp (Schneider & Bitsch 2021), Bitsch & Battistini (2020), Thiabaud et al. (2015), Marboeuf et al. (2014), Mollière et al.

2. **Feeding zones and bulk composition assignment:**
   - How is the composition of a forming planet determined from where it accreted material?
   - Feeding zone analysis in N-body simulations
   - Tracing elemental abundances (C/O ratio, Mg/Si, Fe/Si) from stellar composition through disk to planet

3. **The Drazkowska et al. (2023) A&A 679 L7 paper:**
   - What did it find about pebble vs planetesimal roles in giant planet composition?
   - How does it exemplify the coupling of chemistry + dynamics?
   - Follow-up work and context

4. **Stellar composition → planet composition pipeline:**
   - How does host star composition (measured via spectroscopy) propagate to disk and planet composition?
   - PLATO will provide precise stellar parameters — how does this help?
   - Denisenkov/Adibekyan work on galactic chemical evolution and planet occurrence

5. **PLATO mission science case:**
   - What will PLATO measure? (transit + radial velocity → mass, radius, age, stellar params)
   - How do these measurements constrain formation models?
   - What predictions should formation models make for PLATO targets?

6. **GREP project context:**
   - Search for publications from the Centre for Planetary Habitability (Oslo)
   - Any papers by the GREP team or affiliated researchers
   - What "next level" of modeling do they envision?
